## üéØ PRACTICE TIPS

### 1. **Record Yourself**
- Use phone camera
- Practice each Q&A
- Watch body language (confident? nervous?)
- Note filler words ("um", "eh", "seperti")

### 2. **Time Your Responses**
- Aim for 30-60 seconds per answer
- Too short = not thorough
- Too long = boring/overwhelming

### 3. **Practice with Friend**
- Have them ask questions randomly
- Respond naturally (no script reading)
- Get feedback on clarity & confidence

### 4. **Prepare Transition Phrases**
Use these to buy thinking time:
- "That's a great question..."
- "I'm glad you asked that..."
- "Let me explain..."
- "Here's how I see it..."

### 5. **Body Language**
- Maintain eye contact (shows confidence)
- Open posture (no crossed arms)
- Use hand gestures (but not excessive)
- Smile naturally (relatable, not salesy)

### 6. **Listen Actively**
- Don't interrupt
- Nod while they talk
- Paraphrase their concern ("So you're worried about X?")
- Address the REAL concern (not surface objection)
